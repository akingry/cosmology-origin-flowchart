The Origin of the Universe Accordingto Cosmology and Information Theory

OBSERVABLE STARTING POINT

Galaxies show a redshift-distance relation: the visible universe is expanding.
Prime evidence: Hubble 1929.

QUESTION

Is the visible universe steady-state in ordinary 3D space?

RULED OUT — Yes

A classical steady-state visible universe is ruled out by observed expansion.

SURVIVING PATH / EARLY HOT, DENSE STATE

The visible universe is not steady-state; expansion requires a past denser state. The cosmic microwave background confirms that the visible universe was once hot and dense.
Alpher, Bethe & Gamow 1948; Penzias & Wilson 1965.

QUESTION

Did the visible universe have an early boundary state?

RULED OUT — No

Denying an early boundary state is ruled out by CMB and hot-dense early-state evidence.

SURVIVING PATH / EARLY HOT, DENSE STATE

The Big Bang model describes the visible universe evolving from an extremely hot, dense primordial state. The cosmic microwave background confirms that the visible universe was once hot and dense.
Alpher, Bethe & Gamow 1948; Penzias & Wilson 1965.

QUESTION

Did the visible universe begin from absolute nothing?

RULED OUT — Yes

Creation from absolute nothing is ruled out by the laws of thermodynamics.

SURVIVING PATH

The origin must derive from a prior or deeper source rather than nonbeing.

QUESTION

Does the zero-energy universe proposal
solve the origin problem?
Proposal: positive matter energy balanced
by negative gravitational energy, yielding net zero.
Tryon 1973; Vilenkin 1982.

RULED OUT — Yes

The claim that the zero-energy proposal solves origins is ruled out by accelerating expansion, which shows there is not a cosmic balance.
Riess et al. 1998; Perlmutter et al. 1999.

QUESTION

Do Big Bang/Big Crunch cyclic models
solve the origin problem?

RULED OUT — Yes

Cyclic Big Bang/Big Crunch models are ruled out by accelerating expansion, entropy buildup across cycles, and the unresolved bounce-mechanism problem. They also fail the information/structure reset problem: if information survives, entropy carries forward; if erased, erasure has thermodynamic cost.
Tolman 1934; Landauer 1961; Novello & Bergliaffa 2008; Riess et al. 1998; Perlmutter et al. 1999.

SURVIVING PATH

Brane/bulk cosmologies place our visible universe as a lower-dimensional brane within a higher-dimensional bulk.
Randall & Sundrum 1999; Steinhardt & Turok 2002.

QUESTION

Is information steady-state?

INFORMATION IS STEADY STATE — Yes

Ruled out: expansion and the established early boundary condition show visible-universe information is not steady-state.

INFORMATION CONSTRAINT

Changing or erasing information has energy cost. Intelligence as compression is grounded in algorithmic information theory. An ultimate source must be informationally complete.
Landauer 1961; Hutter 2005.

CONCLUSION

Matter comes from energy → energy from the primordial boundary/singularity → that state from a deeper extradimensional source. The Total Universe = visible + extradimensional. The visible universe is not steady-state, but the whole Universe can be steady-state/eternal. Because information requires energy to change and intelligence is compression, the ultimate source must also be informationally complete. The theological allegory for these states are: omnipresent, omnipotent, and omniscient.
